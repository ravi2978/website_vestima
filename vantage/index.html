The problem

You are personally accountable —
yet dependent on the people you suspect.

01 · Insider threat

Discretion without oversight

Dealers and fund managers exercise wide latitude every second of the trading day. Manual review cannot keep pace — and won't catch what it is looking at.

02 · Broker collusion

External relationships, invisible patterns

Front-running, coordinated placement, and IOC manipulation often involve brokers. Without exchange-direct reconciliation, these patterns stay hidden.

03 · No CEO visibility

Self-reporting is not a control

The leadership is accountable to investors and the regulator — yet has no independent feed of what actually happened on the desk today.
